Define, identify, and classify economic transactions into components of the financial statements (such as revenues, expenses, assets, liabilities and equity), and be able to explain their inter-relationships.
Explain and apply the accounting methods to record basic transactions and other events for businesses using the principles of double entry accounting.
Prepare, analyse and interpret the following three general purpose financial statements and related disclosures for a company: income statement; balance sheet; and cash flow statement.
Evaluate the appropriateness of accounting policies used to prepare financial statements and understand that accounting involves the application of significant professional judgement.
Demonstrate problem solving and critical thinking skills in relation to accounting applications and practices.
Discuss and synthesise information relating to national and international developments in accounting standard setting, accounting research, and media reports in order to understand how real world companies are impacted by accounting.
Record business transactions using accounting software.
